Serialist Origami

Grace CHAN
"Serialist Origami", 2020
Three triads folded from uncut square paper of 210mm x 210mm, logic coded in Processing

“Serialist Origami” is an origami piece that adopts an untraditional approach to paper folding, arranging the steps by serialism. The arrangement of folding steps are not determined by the intended appearance of the final piece, but rather by a boolean flowchart cycle. The decisions within the structure is controlled by chaos theory. The artwork ventures to break through conventional origami that follows symmetry, whether representational or geometric. It explores new possibilities of origami that focuses on procedure and emphasizes the nature of different types of folds, seeing the means as the ends.

Twelve types of common folding techniques are defined as a series of objects.

The types of origami folds are then divided into three categories. From these three categories will be created the three triads, thus giving each triad its unique style and characteristics from the types of folds.
